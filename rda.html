Not-For-Profit Website Redesign:

Riding For The Disabled SA

Client

Riding For The Disabled

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

3 weeks

Tools

Figma, Photoshop, Miro

The Problem

There are various motivations to donate time or money to a not-for-profit or charity, with some people never completing this task. How could we provide the simplest process to complete a financial donation online and an enriching experience for those that want to support our community and inspire others to do the same?

The Solution

Considering the website is out of date and extremely difficult to use we aspire to create an inspiring and informative digital space for people to contribute to the RDA SA community. This will be achieved through a concise online donation and volunteering process to support those living with a disability by allowing them to engage in therapeutic and developmental horse riding experiences.

1. Research & Empathize

  • To begin the process of research and empathy we decided it was essential to understand the strategic goals of the organisation and what they believe they do. We achieved this by contacting the organisation to arrange a meeting to talk to a key stakeholder.

  • This was followed by conducting methods of research to further understand the habits, motivations and past experiences of individuals who have donated to charities.

  • A survey was conducted to gather quantitative data, along with qualitative data from five users, aged 21-56, all of whom are tech savvy and have had experience donating to a charity.

About RDA SA

  • About RDA SA & Stakeholder Message

  • Riding for the Disabled South Australia (RDA SA) is a respected, inclusive NDIS registered organisation delivering a range of equestrian programs for people requiring therapy through sport.

    The organisation is relies 100% on volunteers and donations are divided equally amongst all centres.

    RDA SA requires its website to be informative, reliable and trustworthy for volunteers, donors and program users.

Surveys & Interviews

  • Shaun is a corporate board member of a large Australian energy company.

    Shaun is tasked with visiting the Clean Energy Regulator website to gather knowledge about the Quarterly Carbon Market Report, Carbon Credits, and current Carbon Abatement Projects.

Competitor Analysis

  • We drew on a lot of inspiration from analysing other notably large not-for-profit organizations.


    We also researched previous charity studies with one in particular by Jakob Nielson.

    The study uncovered that if users were unable to decifer the organizations mission, goals, objectives, and work, or how it uses donations and contributions, they were unlikely to donate to them at all, or in very small one-off amounts.

Heuristic Evaluation

  • The website underwent a heuristic evaluation to gain a deeper understanding of where there might be usability issues.

    The efficiency, appearance, and navigation of the website proved to satisfy the criteria the least.

User Testing

  • 5 second testing was conducted on the current website using five tech savvy users between the ages of 21-56.


    We asked users to describe the home page in a few words with most saying that the website looked terrible, outdated and untrustworthy.

    Most did not recognise the site as an NFP organisation and the most alarming reaction was that none of our user tester’s would donate their time or money to this site based on it’s appearance and the lack of information provided regarding funds distribution, which was consistent with prior research.

Sitemap

  • The current layout of the sitemap offers the user a confusing experience. Too many options in the top navigation and a messy, unclear lack of heirarchy.

    It was clear to us we needed to tidy up the overall website and implement a streamlined donation and volunteering process.

    We wanted to champion the organizations mission while offering transparency in donation allocation.

Key Issues Identified

Site is significantly outdated and difficult to navigate

The site fails more than two-thirds of accessibility and usability testing

Concerning number of users unaware of what the site is for, or that it is a charity

Zero users were willing to donate once finding out this was a charity

Solutions

Entire site rebuild to offer an informative and modernised look

Champion accessibility across the website to enable fast and efficient access

Restructuring the sitemap to streamline important user flows

Create a sense of trust and credibility throughout the website

Storyboard

Donation User Flow

  • With the information gathered from our research and an understanding of the typical user we were able to put together a simplified, yet engaging user flow.

Wireframing

  • Wireframing at low, mid fidelity using very basic shapes and contrasts then moving on to high fidelity using the newly curated style guide.


  • Testing at a range of fidelities assured a cheap, fast and reliable way of visualising the final product while allowing for necessary iterations along the way.


Style Guide

  • Using the RDA SA logo as a reference, the colour palette for the website was chosen. A primary purple at the forefront, with complimentary greens in support.


  • Roboto has been sourced as the typeface as it is clean, legible and extremely common, giving a sense of familiarity to site guests.


4. Prototype, Test & Iterate

  • Pushing the wireframes up to high fidelity and repeating the testing process. Iteration on any findings and next steps.


  • Using the style guide the website wireframes begin to look and feel like a fresh, real site. A prototype is created from the high fidelity frames and we will perform similar tests to before to see where the site has improved for our users experience.


    Iteration will take place on any fresh feedback gained from observation of the user tests.

High Fidelity Testing

  • Testing was repeated at high fidelity to discover how effectively the new website overcomes the key issues we had previously identified.


  • The testing was incredibly successful with an exceptional turnaround in the results. Every user immediately know what RDA SA's values were and what the charity represented. They also agreed they would now trust this charity for a donation or to volunteer their time.


Iterations

  • Throughout various testing fidelities there were several substantial iterations that took place.


  • • Notable is the top navigation stretching out across the entire page to create more space between the buttons.

    • The volunteer button having a dark, pronounced purple that jumps out at the user, showing it's importance.

    • Lastly adding icons to the payment options to offer some extra guidance and credibility to the payments.

Lessons Learned & Next Steps

  • RDA SA was a fantastic opptunity to implement various core UX and UI strategies from early research, through to high-fidelity prototyping. Having regular contact with the companies key stakeholders was essential to understanding the charities message, desires and needs. Seeing the power of UX research in uncovering prominent user pain points was incredibly insightful. Using our research data we were able to implement incredibly successful solutions to the websites biggest problems, resulting in a rewarding outcome for the stakeholder, and the group.


    • Next steps:

    • Stakeholder feedback that the website adequately portrays the organization

    • Further redesigning more of the websites core pages

    • Increase information offered to the user on the donation and volunteer flows

    • Repeate the testing and iteration of the site as new features and pages are introduced
